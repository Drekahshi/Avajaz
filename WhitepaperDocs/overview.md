**AVAJAZ: Avalanche-Native Gamified Impact Coordination Protocol**  
**A People-Powered Green Economy for Kenya’s Community Forest Associations & CBOs**


## Executive Summary

AVAJAZ is a decentralized, Avalanche-native impact coordination protocol that transforms the ecological work of Kenya’s Community Forest Associations (CFAs) and Community-Based Organizations (CBOs) into verifiable, market-ready economic assets.  

Kenya’s CFAs and CBOs are already doing the work — seedling nurseries propagate certified native species, agroforestry groups plant and maintain trees on forest edges and degraded land, beekeeping cooperatives depend on intact forest habitat for honey production, and ecotourism user groups generate income from biodiversity and canopy cover. These are government-registered, active community enterprises operating under Kenya Forest Service (KFS) frameworks.  

The problem is not effort — it is invisibility. None of this work is documented in a form that premium markets, carbon registries, ESG buyers, or climate finance institutions can read, verify, or reward.  

AVAJAZ builds the infrastructure that makes CBO ecological work economically legible — turning community forest stewardship into traceable digital assets on Avalanche, verified impact into premium market access, and daily conservation work into dignified income.  

**Hackathon MVP scope (fully implementable):**  
- Activity registration (trees + nursery batches + hive locations)  
- Autonomous AI agent photo credibility verification  
- Verification state-machine + NFT mint trigger  
- Basic Chess Staking + reward distribution  

Roadmap targets: 50 000 verified trees + 500 nursery batches + 200 hive locations in Year 1 pilot across 2–3 CFAs → national CFA network → pan-African verified forest economy.  

AVAJAZ is the economic infrastructure layer for Kenya’s 600+ registered CFAs — converting government-recognized community stewardship into verified, market-ready assets on Avalanche.  
“CBO members are already doing the work. AVAJAZ makes the world pay for it.”

AVAJAZ is deliberately long-term Avalanche-native. Phase 3 envisions an AVAJAZ Impact Subnet dedicated to forest ESG data feeds and KFS integrations.

## The Problem

### 3.1 Jaza Miti’s Verification Gap  
Jaza Miti has institutional legitimacy but no scalable ground-truth layer. CBO-planted trees and nursery batches are rarely confirmed.

### 3.2 CIFOR-ICRAF’s Unimplemented Vision  
Research proves forest products must be traceable economic assets — yet no operational bridge exists for CFAs.

### 3.3 CBOs Are Invisible to Premium Markets  
CFA members practice sustainable forestry by condition of their KFS registration, but cannot prove it. Forest honey sells at generic prices. Ecotourism lacks credentials. Premiums are lost.

### 3.4 Government Recognition Without Economic Reward  
CFAs are monitored — yet this status generates no market advantage. AVAJAZ converts legal recognition into blockchain-verified economic power.

## The Solution: AVAJAZ

**Layer 1 — Proof of Impact (Verification Engine)**  
Multi-stage lifecycle verification adapted for all four CBO activities. Nursery groups verify batches, planting groups confirm survival, beekeeping groups document hive health, ecotourism groups verify biodiversity. All verification earns AVAX rewards and runs on standard smartphones. Autonomous AI agents (0xGasless AgentKit) perform real-time photo credibility checks (deepfake detection, perceptual hashing, C2PA, GPS validation).

**Layer 2 — CBO Products as Assets (Asset Layer)**  
Every verified activity generates a permanent Avalanche digital twin — minted as NFT with auditable provenance. Nursery batches, honey harvests, ecotourism experiences, and standing tree inventories become market-ready assets.

**Layer 3 — Gamified Coordination (Engagement Engine)**  
Four capital-preserving skill-based games (Chess Staking, Community Prediction Markets, Chrono Siege, Shadow Syndicate) sustain daily engagement between seasonal cycles. No member ever loses principal stake.
